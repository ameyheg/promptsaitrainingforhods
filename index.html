Writing Effective Prompts
1

Role

Tell the AI what role it should take.
"Act like a sr. business analyst"

2

Context

Share your situation so AI understands better.
"I have 30 min to review data"

3

Task

Clearly state what you want the AI to do.
"Summarize and highlight trends"

4

Example

Share a reference if you have one.
"Like the monthly executive summary"

5

Format

Specify how you want the output.
"Short bullets" or "Table with 2 cols"

6

Tone

Mention the tone you want.
"Professional but friendly"

Safety, Accuracy & Best Practices

✅ Best Practices

  • Protect personal information: Do not enter client names, phone numbers, photos, Aadhaar or any identifiable details into AI tools.
  • Avoid sharing confidential designs: Remove part numbers or unique identifiers before entering technical details.
  • Always double-check AI answers: AI can make mistakes. Use your own judgement for accuracy.
  • Use AI for support, not final decisions: Critical decisions must follow your organisation policy & guidelines.

🚫 What NOT to Share

  • 🔴 Images/drawings of confidential or patented designs
  • 🔴 Proprietary designs or specifications
  • 🔴 Phone numbers or WhatsApp chats
  • 🔴 Internal confidential business documents
  • 🔴 Documents with staff names, salaries, or confidential info

✅ What is Generally Safe

  • 🟢 Fictional examples
  • 🟢 Rough notes
  • 🟢 SOP steps
  • 🟢 General process descriptions
  • 🟢 Public information

⚠️ AI Hallucinations

What are they? The AI gives an answer that looks correct but is actually wrong or partly made up.
Why it happens: AI tries to fill missing information or guesses when the prompt is unclear.

  • Use Thinking mode for step-by-step reasoning
  • Ask the AI to show its sources or cite references
  • Cross-check answers with trusted sources or a senior colleague
  • If unsure, do not use the AI output for critical decisions
